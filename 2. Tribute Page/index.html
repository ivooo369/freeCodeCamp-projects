Steve Jobs

The man who changed the world of technology!

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs's life:

  • 1955 - Born on 24th of February 1955 in San Francisco, California.
  • 1973 - Attends, but later drops out of Reed College. Then he starts to travel through India until 1974. During his time in India, Steve Jobs becomes more interested in Buddhism.
  • 1975 - After coming back from traveling through India, Steve Jobs starts working on Apple I Computer together with Steve Woz. Steve Wozniak attends the first meeting of the Homebrew Computer Club in Gordon French's garage on March 5, 1975. He is so moved that he begins to work on what would become the Apple I computer right away. After creating one for himself and demonstrating it to the club, he and Steve Jobs distribute schematics (technical designs) for the computer to interested club members, as well as assisting some of them in building and testing prototypes. Then Steve Jobs proposed that they develop and sell a single etched and silkscreened circuit board just the bare board, no electronic components that customers could use to make computers. Wozniak predicts that laying out the board design would cost $1,000, with production costing a further $20 per board. The start of selling their first computer was set into motion.
  • 1976 - On the 1st of April 1976 Apple Inc. is incorporated by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ron Wayne. The Apple I goes on sale in July 1976 for $666.66 because Wozniak "loved repeating numbers" and because the wholesale price of $500 increased by a third. The first units produced were used in high school math classes and donated to Liza Loop's Public Access Computer Center. About 200 were produced and all but 25 were sold within 9 or 10 months.
  • 1984 - Macintosh is launched at Apple's annual shareholder meeting.
  • 1985 - Leaves Apple and starts a new computer firm NeXT.
  • 1986 - Buys Pixar Animation Studios.
  • 1991 - Marries Laurene Powel.
  • 1993 - Toy Story work is haulted and it becomes NeXT Software Inc.
  • 1996 - Apple Inc. announces the acquisition of NeXT.
  • 2000 - He is back as CEO at Apple Inc.
  • 2001 - First iPod is introduces and Apple opens retail stores.
  • 2005 - Gives commencement speech at Stanford University. In the same year Steve Jobs unveils iWork, the Mac mini, iPod shuffle and Mac OS X 10.4.
  • 2007 - Introduction of iPhone, Apple TV and iPod Touch. Apple Computers becomes Apple Inc.
  • 2009 - Takes medical leave for six months and has surgery for liver transplant.
  • 2010 - Introduces iPad, iPhone 4, iPod Touch 4G and new Macbook Air.
  • 2011 - Resignes his position as CEO of Apple and dies shortly afterwards.
